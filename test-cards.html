Climate Change Debate Cards

Climate change poses an existential threat to humanity and requires immediate action

Smith 2023 - Climate scientist at MIT, published in Nature Climate

The evidence is overwhelming that human activities are causing rapid climate change. Temperature records show that the past decade has been the warmest on record, with unprecedented increases in global average temperatures. Ice sheets in Greenland and Antarctica are melting at accelerating rates, contributing to rising sea levels that threaten coastal communities worldwide.

Renewable energy transition is economically viable and creates jobs

Johnson and Lee 2024 - Economic researchers, Journal of Sustainable Development

Our analysis demonstrates that transitioning to renewable energy sources is not only environmentally necessary but also economically beneficial. The solar and wind energy sectors have created over 3 million jobs in the past five years. Moreover, the cost of renewable energy has dropped by 80% since 2010, making it competitive with or cheaper than fossil fuels in most markets.
